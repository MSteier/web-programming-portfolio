Russell's Viper

Russell's Viper (Daboia russelii). Named after scotish Herpatologist Patrick Russell, the Russell's Viper belongs to the Viperidae family. This highly venomous animal is one of 52 species native to india.The genus name derived from from the Hindi word meaning "that lies hid", or "the lurker". the Russells viper is known for it's loud hiss which has been compared to that of an air compressor. Although its diet is most comprised of rodents, it has been know to also eat scorpions, land crabs and small reptiles. |Return to map|
