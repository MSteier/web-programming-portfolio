Western Diamondback (Crotalus atrox). The Western Diamondback is common in the western and southwestern United States and is a member of the viper familiy the western diamondback typically grows to 4 feet in length however, although rare, larger rattlesnakes have been captured. Rattlesnakes have been known to feed on eats small mammals such as chipmunks, prairie dogs, gophers, ground squirrels, rabbits, mice and rats.
